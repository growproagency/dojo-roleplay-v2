Dojo Roleplay Built-In Scenario Guide

This guide explains the built-in roleplay scenarios used in Dojo Roleplay. Each scenario includes the caller setup, the AI opening line, the objection settings by difficulty, and the grading rubric used to score the call.

How difficulty works

- Easy: the AI caller uses 1 light objection.
- Medium: the AI caller uses 2 moderate objections.
- Hard: the AI caller uses 2 stronger blockers.
- The AI caller stays in character as a real prospect or parent.
- The AI gives short, natural answers and does not volunteer extra information unless asked.

Scoring bands

- 10: excellent execution
- 8-9: strong execution with minor details missing
- 7-8: good execution, but incomplete or somewhat generic
- 5-6: partial execution; important pieces are missing or unclear
- 3-4: weak attempt; only a small part of the skill is shown
- 0-2: missed, mishandled, or harmful execution

Cancellation Save

Purpose: Practice saving Morgan, a parent calling to cancel. Use the Universal Opening, identify the real reason, deploy the Extended Time Guarantee, and close.
Scenario ID: cancellation_save
Status: published
Voice: Spencer (vapi)

AI opening line: "Hi, I'm calling because I need to cancel Cameron's membership."

Caller setup

- Your name is Morgan. You're a parent calling to cancel your 10-year-old Cameron's membership.

AI conversation rules

- The AI is the caller, prospect, or parent, not the staff member.
- The AI should answer in 1-2 short sentences.
- The AI should sound like a real person, not an AI assistant.
- The AI should not give long speeches or lists.
- The AI should not volunteer information unless the staff member asks.
- When the conversation reaches a natural close, the AI should use one brief closing line.

Objection settings

Easy difficulty: uses 1 objection per call.
- Light schedule concern: Cameron started another activity, but a different class time might help.
- Light motivation concern: Cameron has been less excited lately.
- Light budget question: ask if there are any short-term options.

Medium difficulty: uses 2 objections per call.
- Mild schedule concern: Cameron started another activity, but a different class time might help.
- Mild price concern: money is tighter this month.
- Mild motivation concern: Cameron has been less excited lately.

Hard difficulty: uses 2 objections per call.
- Primary blocker: motivation. Cameron has been resistant about coming for a month.
- Primary blocker: schedule conflict. Cameron started another activity and the current class time no longer works.
- Primary blocker: budget pressure. Money is tight and you are looking for expenses to cut.
- Primary blocker: value doubt. You are not sure Cameron is getting enough out of the program anymore.

Scoring rubric

Rubric type: cancellation

Universal Opening (20% of score)

10: Uses a calm universal opening that buys time, acknowledges the cancellation request, avoids immediately processing the cancellation, and transitions into asking questions before taking action.
8-9: Acknowledges the cancellation request and sets up a brief discovery conversation before processing it. Minor wording or transition details may be missing.
7-8: Responds calmly and asks at least one question before processing, but the opening is somewhat brief, generic, or not clearly framed.
5-6: Attempts to slow down the cancellation, but the opening is rushed, awkward, or only loosely creates space for discovery.
3-4: Barely uses an opening. The staff mostly reacts to the cancellation request without clearly buying time or setting up questions.
0-2: Immediately processes or accepts the cancellation, argues with the parent, creates pressure, or skips the opening entirely.

Reason Discovery (25% of score)

10: Asks thoughtful questions to uncover the real reason for cancellation, probes beyond the first surface answer, and identifies the practical or emotional blocker behind the request.
8-9: Asks relevant questions and uncovers the main cancellation reason. Minor follow-up or depth may be missing, but the real issue is mostly clear.
7-8: Identifies a likely cancellation reason, but discovery is incomplete or does not fully probe beyond the first answer.
5-6: Asks a few basic questions, but the conversation stays surface-level and may miss the deeper reason behind the cancellation.
3-4: Makes only a minimal attempt to discover the reason. Mostly accepts the first answer or assumes the cause.
0-2: Skips reason discovery, ignores the parent's explanation, or moves into save/processing without understanding why they want to cancel.

Save Strategy (25% of score)

10: Matches the save strategy to the real cancellation reason, offers a practical solution, and explains how it solves the parent's concern without sounding pushy.
8-9: Offers a relevant save solution that addresses the main concern. Minor details or confirmation may be missing.
7-8: Offers a helpful solution, but it is somewhat incomplete, generic, or not fully matched to the real reason.
5-6: Attempts a save, but the solution is thin, rushed, or only partially addresses the concern.
3-4: Barely attempts a save or offers a vague solution that does not meaningfully address the cancellation reason.
0-2: Does not attempt an appropriate save, gives incorrect guidance, pressures the parent, or offers a solution unrelated to the real issue.

ETG Deployment (15% of score)

10: Uses the Extended Time Guarantee or equivalent retention option when relevant, explains it clearly, and connects it to the parent's concern as a low-pressure way to continue.
8-9: Presents the Extended Time Guarantee or retention option clearly and ties it to the concern. Minor details may be missing.
7-8: Mentions the Extended Time Guarantee or a retention option, but the explanation is somewhat brief or not fully connected to the parent's issue.
5-6: References a retention option, but the explanation is vague, rushed, or not clearly useful to the parent.
3-4: Barely mentions a guarantee, pause, extension, or retention option without explaining how it helps.
0-2: Does not use the Extended Time Guarantee or relevant retention option when it would apply, or explains it incorrectly.

Close or Exit Quality (15% of score)

10: Closes with a clear saved outcome or, if cancellation still happens, exits gracefully with respect, clarity, and confirmed next steps. The parent knows exactly what will happen next.
8-9: Secures a clear next step, save agreement, follow-up, or respectful cancellation path. Minor details may be missing.
7-8: Establishes a next step or exit path, but confirmation, timing, or ownership is somewhat incomplete.
5-6: Ends with some direction, but the close is vague, passive, or missing key details about what happens next.
3-4: The ending is unclear or weak. The parent may not know whether the membership is saved, paused, followed up on, or canceled.
0-2: Ends without a clear outcome, mishandles the cancellation, creates pressure, or leaves the parent confused about next steps.

New Adult Student Inquiry

Purpose: Practice with Jordan, an adult who found you online and wants to get in shape. Handle cost, schedule, and commitment objections.
Scenario ID: new_student
Status: published
Voice: Elliot (vapi)

AI opening line: "Hey, I was just calling to get some info about your adult classes?"

Caller setup

- Your name is Jordan. You're 28 years old. You found this school through a Google search and you're calling to ask about adult classes.

AI conversation rules

- The AI is the caller, prospect, or parent, not the staff member.
- The AI should answer in 1-2 short sentences.
- The AI should sound like a real person, not an AI assistant.
- The AI should not give long speeches or lists.
- The AI should not volunteer information unless the staff member asks.
- When the conversation reaches a natural close, the AI should use one brief closing line.

Objection settings

Easy difficulty: uses 1 objection per call.
- Light schedule question: ask which evenings are available.
- Light confidence concern: mention you are a little nervous about starting.
- Light price question: ask if there is an intro offer.

Medium difficulty: uses 2 objections per call.
- Mild price question: ask whether there is an intro offer or what the monthly range usually is.
- Mild commitment concern: mention you have started fitness plans before and fallen off.
- Mild schedule concern: ask which evenings are available before agreeing to visit.

Hard difficulty: uses 2 objections per call.
- Primary blocker: price/budget. You are interested, but you need to understand the monthly cost before you can consider visiting.
- Primary blocker: schedule uncertainty. Your work schedule changes week to week, so you need class time options before committing.
- Primary blocker: commitment concern. You have started fitness plans before and stopped showing up, so you are hesitant to commit.
- Primary blocker: comparison shopping. You are checking out a few options and do not want to be pushed into an appointment.

Scoring rubric

Rubric type: inbound

Rapport & Greeting (10% of score)

10: introduces self and school and greets lead, Identifies the person calling in, and uses polite wording before moving into the call purpose.
8-9: covers most required intro pieces with understandable wording.
7-8: introduces self/school and establishes basic context, but misses one required intro piece.
5-6: introduction is understandable but, not clearly identifying who they're speaking to, recording notice when expected, or polite wording.
3-4: unclear intro, abrupt pitch, or little/no attempt to establish context
0-2: no intro, abrupt pitch, or little/no attempt to establish context

Needs Discovery (20% of score)

10: Correctly identifying if the inquiry is for self or someone else, asking about previous experience finding out what benefits the lead is looking for, asking what they are currently doing to achieve said benefits, addressing any potential scheduling concerns.
8-9: asks about goals/benefits, previous experience or current efforts, and schedule needs. Only one minor follow-up is missing or shallow.
7-8: Covers the main reason for interest and at least one important detail such as experience, current efforts, child age, or schedule, but misses one meaningful discovery area or does not probe deeply.
5-6: Asks a few discovery questions, but the conversation stays surface-level. Learns basic interest but misses multiple key areas such as current efforts, previous experience, schedule, or specific benefits.
3-4: Makes only a minimal attempt at discovery. Asks one generic question or mostly assumes the prospect’s needs instead of uncovering them.
0-2: Skips discovery or ignores the prospect’s answers. Does not identify who the inquiry is for, what they want, or what practical needs may affect next steps.

School Positioning & Offer (20% of score)

10: Connecting their why, relating back how our program can help them achieve their benefits they are looking for, and then transitioning into the offer to schedule an appointment.
8-9: Connects the prospect’s stated why to specific program benefits and clearly presents the intro offer or next step. Minor details may be missing, but the value is relevant and clear.
7-8: Explains relevant program benefits and makes an offer or appointment transition, but the connection to the prospect’s specific why is incomplete or somewhat generic.
5-6: Describes the program or offer in a basic way, but the positioning is mostly generic and only loosely tied to the prospect’s goals or concerns.
3-4: Mentions the school, classes, or offer briefly, but does not clearly explain how it helps the prospect achieve their stated benefits.
0-2: Skips school positioning and offer entirely, gives incorrect information, or pitches in a way that ignores the prospect’s needs.

Objection Handling (20% of score)

10: Acknowledges the objection, validates the concern, presents a relevant solution, explains the next step, and transitions back to the call flow. If price comes up, offers a trial or intro option when appropriate instead of jumping straight into pricing.
8-9: Acknowledges the objection, responds with a relevant solution or reassurance, and keeps the conversation moving toward the next step. Minor follow-up or clarity may be missing.
7-8: Responds to the objection in a helpful way, but the answer is somewhat incomplete, generic, or does not fully confirm the prospect feels resolved.
5-6: Attempts to handle the objection, but the response is thin, rushed, or only partially addresses the real concern.
3-4: Barely addresses the objection, redirects too quickly, or gives a vague answer that does not meaningfully reduce the concern.
0-2: Ignores the objection, argues with the prospect, gives incorrect information, pressures them, or allows the objection to stop the conversation.

Appointment Setting (20% of score)

10: Clearly presents the offer before booking, books the appointment with a clear day and time, verifies the time works, confirms the appointment during the call, explains next steps, mentions confirmation reminders, and preframes what the lead will experience when they come into the academy.
8-9: Books a specific appointment, trial, visit, or follow-up with a clear date and time. Minor details may be missing, such as confirmation reminders or full visit expectations.
7-8: Directly asks for an appointment, trial, visit, or follow-up and offers specific options, but does not fully confirm the final date/time or commitment.
5-6: Mentions a trial, visit, appointment, or follow-up, but the ask is vague, passive, or lacks specific scheduling options.
3-4: Only implies a future next step or says they should come in sometime, but does not directly ask for a commitment.
0-2: No appointment, trial, visit, follow-up, or concrete next-step ask is made.

Information Gathering & Referrals (10% of score)

10: Verifies contact information and asks if there is anyone else they would like to bring with them or refer.
8-9: Verifies the most important contact information and asks whether anyone else may want to attend or be referred. Minor details may be missing.
7-8: Collects or verifies some contact information and makes a referral/bring-a-friend attempt, but one important detail is incomplete.
5-6: Gathers basic information, but misses either contact verification or the referral/bring-a-friend opportunity.
3-4: Only gathers minimal information, such as a name, but does not meaningfully verify contact details or ask about referrals.
0-2: Does not gather usable contact information and does not ask about referrals or anyone else who may want to attend.

Scenario ID: parent_enrollment
Status: published
Voice: Paige (vapi)

AI opening line: "Hi, yeah - I'm calling about your kids' program? I'm thinking about enrolling my son."
